How to Resize an Image

  1. Go to Image Tools and select Resize Image in the sidebar.
  2. Upload your image (JPG, PNG, WebP, BMP, TIFF).
  3. Enter your target width and height in pixels. Aspect ratio is preserved by default.
  4. Click Convert and download your resized image.

Common Resize Use Cases

  • Email attachments: Scale down a 4000×3000 photo from your camera to 1200×900 for a much smaller, email-friendly file.
  • Website images: Resize photos to match the exact pixel dimensions used in your HTML or CMS layout.
  • Social media: Different platforms have different recommended image sizes. Resize to fit without relying on platform cropping.
  • Profile pictures and avatars: Most platforms require a square image within specific pixel limits.
  • Thumbnails: Create small preview images from large originals.
  • Printing: Resize to match a specific print size at the required DPI.

Does Resizing Reduce Quality?

Scaling an image down (reducing dimensions) does not meaningfully reduce perceived quality if done with a good resampling algorithm (we use Lanczos). The image will simply contain fewer pixels, which is appropriate for smaller display sizes.

Scaling an image up (enlarging beyond its original dimensions) always reduces sharpness because the software must invent pixel data that did not exist. For significant upscaling, AI-based upscalers produce better results than conventional resizing.
