How to Trim an Audio File

  1. Go to Audio Tools and select Trim Audio in the sidebar.
  2. Upload your audio file (any of the 7 supported formats).
  3. Enter a start time and end time. You can use MM:SS format (e.g. 1:30 for 1 minute 30 seconds) or plain seconds (e.g. 90).
  4. Click Convert. The output file contains only the audio between your start and end points.
  5. Download your trimmed clip.

Time Format Examples

  • 0 or 0:00 — Start from the beginning
  • 30 — 30 seconds in
  • 1:30 — 1 minute 30 seconds
  • 10:00 — 10 minutes in
  • 1:23:45 — 1 hour, 23 minutes, 45 seconds

Common Use Cases

  • Create a ringtone: Cut the best 30 seconds from a song.
  • Remove silence from recordings: Trim dead air from the start and end of voice recordings.
  • Extract a clip from a podcast or lecture: Pull out a specific segment to share or reference.
  • Sample extraction: Cut a specific passage from a longer recording for use in a music project.
  • Remove intros/outros: Strip repeated intro music or sign-off segments from a series of recordings.

Does Trimming Reduce Quality?

PDFWorks uses stream copy for trimming when possible — copying the audio data without re-encoding. This is instantaneous and results in no quality loss. For formats where stream copy is not possible, a re-encode is used with the same codec and bitrate as the source.
