Which LUFS Target Should I Use?

  • −14 LUFS — Spotify, YouTube, Amazon Music, Tidal. The standard target for music distribution on streaming platforms.
  • −16 LUFS — Podcasts, general online audio, voice content. The recommended level for podcasting and spoken-word distribution.
  • −23 LUFS — EBU R128 broadcast standard. Required for television and radio broadcast content in Europe.
  • −24 LUFS — ATSC A/85 standard. Used for broadcast in North America.

What Is LUFS?

LUFS (Loudness Units relative to Full Scale) is the standard unit for measuring integrated audio loudness. Unlike simple peak measurements, LUFS accounts for human hearing sensitivity across different frequencies and excludes silences — giving a reliable measure of how loud a programme actually sounds to a listener. Read our full guide to audio normalization and LUFS.

Does Normalization Change the Sound?

Normalization applies a uniform gain adjustment across the entire file — it makes the whole file louder or quieter without changing the relative dynamics. The character, timbre, and dynamics of the audio are preserved. What changes is the absolute level. If the normalization requires a large gain increase and the file has loud peaks, a limiter is applied to prevent digital clipping.
