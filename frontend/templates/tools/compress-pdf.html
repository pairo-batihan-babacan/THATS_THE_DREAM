What Does PDF Compression Do?

PDF compression reduces the size of your PDF file by optimising its internal content. The primary technique is reducing the resolution and re-encoding quality of embedded images, which typically account for 80–95% of a PDF's file size. The process also strips unnecessary metadata and optimises the PDF's internal structure. The result is a smaller file that looks identical on screen and is perfectly suitable for email, web upload, or cloud storage.

Compression Quality Levels Explained

PDFWorks offers three quality levels to suit different use cases:

  • Low quality — Maximum compression: Images are reduced to 72 DPI and re-encoded at JPEG quality 40. Best for PDFs that will only be read on screen and where pixel-perfect image quality isn't required. Typical file size reduction: 70–90%. Ideal for: internal memos, email attachments, web uploads.
  • Medium quality — Recommended: Images are reduced to 120 DPI and re-encoded at JPEG quality 72. The right balance for most documents — visually clean at normal viewing sizes, significantly smaller file size. Typical reduction: 50–75%. Ideal for: most professional documents, presentations, reports.
  • High quality — Light compression: Images are reduced to 200 DPI and re-encoded at JPEG quality 85. Minimal visible quality difference from the original. Typical reduction: 20–40%. Ideal for: documents that may be printed professionally, client-facing materials where visual quality is important.

How Much Will My PDF Shrink?

The actual compression ratio depends heavily on what's inside your PDF:

  • Photo-heavy PDFs (catalogues, portfolios): Often compress 70–90% at medium quality. A 20 MB product catalogue may compress to under 2 MB.
  • Scanned documents: Typically compress 50–80%, depending on the original scan resolution.
  • Presentation PDFs (from PowerPoint): Often compress 40–70%, depending on slide graphics density.
  • Text-only PDFs: Minimal compression, typically 5–20%, since text takes up very little space.

Will Compression Damage My PDF?

Compression is lossy for images within the PDF — there is a slight reduction in image sharpness at lower quality settings. However, at medium quality, this difference is typically invisible at normal screen zoom levels (100%). Text content is never damaged by PDF compression — only embedded images are affected.

Always keep a backup of the original high-resolution PDF. Compressed files cannot be "un-compressed" to recover the original quality.

Technical Details

PDFWorks uses PyMuPDF (a Python binding for MuPDF) to process PDF files. The compression algorithm applies downsampling to embedded images and re-encodes them using JPEG compression at the selected quality level. The PDF structure is then re-optimised to remove redundant data. No content beyond image quality is modified — text, fonts, layout, hyperlinks, and document structure are fully preserved.
