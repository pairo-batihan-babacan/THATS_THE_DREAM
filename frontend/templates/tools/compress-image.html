How to Compress an Image

  3. Set the quality level (1–95). A quality of 75–85 is recommended for most images.
  4. Click Convert and download your compressed image.

How Image Compression Works

For JPEG images, compression reduces file size by discarding fine visual detail that is difficult for the human eye to perceive — particularly in areas of complex texture and at high frequencies. A quality setting of 80 typically produces files 60–80% smaller than the original with no visible difference at normal viewing distances.

PNG uses lossless compression, so reducing the quality level converts a PNG to JPEG internally to achieve size reduction. If you need to keep the PNG format and lossless quality, converting to WebP lossless is a better approach.

Recommended Quality Settings

  • 85–95: Near-lossless. Minimal compression. Good for professional photography where quality must be preserved.
  • 75–85: Excellent quality with significant size reduction. The right range for website images and social media.
  • 60–75: Good quality, smaller files. Suitable for thumbnails, previews, and images viewed at small sizes.
  • Below 60: Visible artefacts. Only appropriate when file size is the absolute priority.

Tips for Best Results

  • For photos, JPEG compression at quality 80 is usually imperceptible and dramatically reduces file size.
  • For images with text, logos, or sharp edges, use PNG or WebP to avoid JPEG block artefacts.
  • Always keep your original — compression is lossy for JPEG and cannot be reversed.
  • For web use, also consider converting to WebP for even smaller files with equivalent quality.
