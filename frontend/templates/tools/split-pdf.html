Split PDF

Extract specific pages from a PDF by specifying a page range. Perfect for separating chapters, extracting sections, or creating smaller PDFs from a large document.

How to Split a PDF on PDFWorks

  1. Go to PDF Tools and select Split PDF in the sidebar.
  2. Upload your PDF file.
  3. Enter the page range you want to extract (e.g., "1-5" for pages 1 through 5, or "3,7,12" for specific pages).
  4. Click Convert. The extracted pages are packaged as a new PDF.
  5. Download your split PDF.

Page Range Formats

You can specify pages in several formats:

  • Range: "1-10" extracts pages 1 through 10.
  • Individual pages: "1,3,5" extracts pages 1, 3, and 5 only.
  • Combined: "1-5,8,12-15" extracts pages 1–5, page 8, and pages 12–15.

Common Use Cases

  • Extracting a single chapter from a large PDF book or manual
  • Isolating specific pages from a report for sharing
  • Separating a multi-document PDF into its components
  • Removing unwanted pages from a scanned document
  • Creating a smaller preview version of a long PDF

Split vs Merge

Split and Merge are complementary operations. Use Split to extract pages; use Merge to combine them. Together, you can reorganise any PDF: split out the parts you need, reorder them, and merge back into a new arrangement.
