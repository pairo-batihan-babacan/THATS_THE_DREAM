Why Convert Word to PDF?

Microsoft Word's DOCX format is excellent for editing but not ideal for sharing. When you send a DOCX file, the recipient's layout may differ from yours: fonts may be substituted if they don't have the same typefaces installed, line breaks may shift, and the document may reflow to different page lengths. Converting to PDF "locks" the layout — the recipient sees exactly what you see, on any device, with any PDF viewer.

Supported File Types

The Word to PDF tool accepts .docx (Word 2007 and later, standard format) and .doc (older Word 97–2003 format). For best results, use DOCX as it's the current standard and converts more accurately.

When to Use Word to PDF

  • Sending final documents — Invoices, contracts, proposals, CVs: anything you're sending as a finished document should be PDF.
  • Submitting to portals — Job applications, planning submissions, tender responses often require PDF format.
  • Printing services — Print shops prefer PDF as it guarantees layout will be as intended.
  • Archiving — PDFs are a better long-term archive format than DOCX, which depends on compatible software to render.
