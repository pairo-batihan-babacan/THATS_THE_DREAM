Supported Input Video Formats

PDFWorks accepts the most common video container formats:

  • MP4 (.mp4) — The most widely used video format. Used by smartphones, YouTube downloads, screen recorders, and video editors. Audio is typically AAC or MP3 inside the container.
  • MOV (.mov) — Apple's QuickTime format. Common output from iPhones, iPads, and macOS screen recordings.
  • MKV (.mkv) — Matroska video container. Popular for high-quality video downloads and media server content. May contain multiple audio tracks.
  • AVI (.avi) — Older Microsoft format still common in legacy archives and some camera systems.
  • WebM (.webm) — Open-source format used by web browsers (Chrome, Firefox) for HTML5 video. Common in browser-based screen recordings.

Supported Output Audio Formats

After extraction, you can download the audio in these formats:

  • MP3 — The universal audio standard. Supported by every device, player, and platform. Ideal for music, podcasts, voice recordings, and anything you need to share widely. Lossy compression; smaller file sizes.
  • AAC (.aac / .m4a) — The successor to MP3, used natively by Apple devices and YouTube. Better audio quality than MP3 at the same bitrate. Excellent choice when the source is already AAC (e.g. MP4 files from iPhones).
  • WAV (.wav) — Uncompressed, lossless audio. Produces the largest files but perfect quality. Best for professional audio editing, sampling, and archiving. No quality loss whatsoever.
  • OGG (.ogg) — Open-source format using Vorbis codec. Good quality-to-size ratio. Supported by Linux, Android, and web browsers. A free alternative to MP3.
  • FLAC (.flac) — Free Lossless Audio Codec. Lossless compression that retains every detail of the original audio, but at about half the file size of WAV. Ideal for archiving high-quality audio or music from lossless video sources.
  • M4A (.m4a) — AAC audio in an MP4 container. The standard format for Apple Music and iTunes. Compatible with all Apple devices and most modern media players.

Which Format Should I Choose?

The right format depends on what you plan to do with the audio:

  • Sharing or uploading online: Choose MP3. It's supported everywhere and produces compact files.
  • Apple devices or iTunes: Choose M4A or AAC for native compatibility.
  • Professional editing or sampling: Choose WAV for zero quality loss.
  • Long-term archiving: Choose FLAC — you get lossless quality with smaller files than WAV.
  • Open-source / Linux workflow: Choose OGG.

Quality Levels Explained

Quality settings apply to lossy formats (MP3, AAC, OGG, M4A). WAV and FLAC are always lossless and unaffected by this setting.

  • Low — 96 kbps: Smallest file size. Audible quality reduction, particularly noticeable on music. Suitable for voice recordings, podcasts, and spoken-word content where bandwidth matters.
  • Medium — 128 kbps: The standard quality level. Indistinguishable from the original for most listeners at normal volume. Recommended for general use.
  • High — 192 kbps: Near-transparent quality. Essentially indistinguishable from the original audio track for virtually all listeners. Recommended for music and content where audio quality matters. This is the default setting.

What Happens If My Video Has Multiple Audio Tracks?

Many MKV files and some MP4 files contain multiple audio streams — for example, a film with English and French dubs, or a video with a separate commentary track. PDFWorks automatically extracts the first audio stream from the file, which is typically the primary language track. If you need to extract a specific track, use a desktop tool such as FFmpeg or HandBrake which offer full stream selection control.

Will the Audio Quality Be Reduced?

Extraction involves a re-encoding step for lossy formats (MP3, AAC, OGG, M4A). This means there is a generation of quality loss compared to the original, even at High quality. For WAV and FLAC, re-encoding preserves the full audio waveform with no loss. If you're working with professionally produced video and audio fidelity is critical, choose WAV or FLAC and then convert to your target format in a dedicated audio editor (Audacity, Logic Pro, Adobe Audition) to have full control over encoding parameters.

Technical Details

PDFWorks uses FFmpeg, the industry-standard multimedia processing library, to handle all audio extraction. The extraction pipeline uses the -vn flag to strip the video stream and re-encodes the audio to the selected format and bitrate. FFmpeg supports decoding the audio from virtually every video container and codec in wide use today, including H.264/AAC (MP4), H.265/AAC, VP9/Opus (WebM), and more.
