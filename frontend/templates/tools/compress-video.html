How to Compress a Video Online

  1. Go to Video Tools and select Compress Video in the sidebar.
  2. Upload your video file. Supported input formats: MP4, MOV, MKV, AVI, WebM. Maximum file size: 50 MB.
  3. Select a quality level: High, Medium, or Low.
  4. Click Convert. Processing time varies by file length — typically 15–90 seconds.
  5. Download your compressed MP4.

Quality Levels

  • High (CRF 22): Visually near-lossless. Largest compressed output. Best for archiving or professional content where quality must be preserved.
  • Medium (CRF 28): Good quality with significant size reduction — typically 40–60% smaller than the original. Recommended for sharing and uploading.
  • Low (CRF 32): Maximum compression with visible quality reduction. Best when file size is the absolute priority, such as sending via email or messaging apps.

Output Format: Why Always MP4?

All compressed videos are output as H.264/AAC MP4 — the universal web video format that plays on every browser, phone, TV, and device without plugins or codec installation. Source files in MKV, MOV, AVI, or WebM format are re-encoded into this universally compatible format as part of the compression process.

How Much Smaller Will My Video Get?

Results depend on the source content and original encoding:

  • Camera footage or screen recordings that have not been previously compressed can often be reduced by 60–80% at Medium quality.
  • Already-compressed videos (e.g. MP4 downloaded from a streaming site) may only shrink by 20–40% since the source is already efficient.
  • Screen recordings compress very well due to static areas — often 70–85% smaller at Medium.
  • High-motion content (sport, action) compresses less efficiently than static or slow-moving scenes.

Tips

  • For videos you will upload to YouTube or social media, use Medium or High — the platform will compress further, so starting with a highly compressed file results in double-compression artefacts.
  • For videos to send via email or messaging apps (under 25 MB), use Low quality and ensure the input is under 50 MB.
  • Keep your original video — compression is not reversible.
