How to Change Audio Speed

  1. Go to Audio Tools and select Change Speed in the sidebar.
  2. Upload your audio file (MP3, WAV, FLAC, AAC, OGG, M4A, AIFF).
  3. Enter a speed multiplier: 0.5 for half speed, 1.0 for normal, 1.5 for 1.5× speed, etc.
  4. Click Convert and download your file.

Speed Reference

  • 0.25× — Quarter speed. Very slow, useful for detailed transcription or learning intricate passages.
  • 0.5× — Half speed. Slow enough to catch fast speech or complex music.
  • 0.75× — Slightly slow. Useful for transcription or careful listening.
  • 1.0× — Normal playback speed.
  • 1.25× — 25% faster. Common for podcast consumption. Saves time with minimal perceptual impact.
  • 1.5× — 50% faster. A popular lecture and podcast speed. Most speech remains clearly intelligible.
  • 2.0× — Double speed. Fast speech. Works well for familiar content or slow speakers.
  • 2.0×–4.0× — Very fast. Intelligibility varies by original speaking pace. Useful for scanning content.

Does It Change Pitch?

No. PDFWorks uses time-stretching (the ffmpeg atempo filter), which changes the playback speed without altering the pitch. A podcast sped up to 1.5× will play faster but the voices will sound at their normal pitch — not chipmunk-high or artificially slow. This is different from simply speeding up a tape or playing a record at the wrong speed, which does change pitch.

Common Use Cases

  • Podcasts and lectures: Speed up to 1.5×–2× to consume content faster.
  • Language learning: Slow down native-speaker audio to 0.7×–0.8× for easier comprehension.
  • Transcription: Slow down fast speech to 0.5× for easier typing.
  • Music practice: Slow down a fast passage to practice at tempo.
  • Accessibility: Speed up or slow down content to match a listener's preference.
