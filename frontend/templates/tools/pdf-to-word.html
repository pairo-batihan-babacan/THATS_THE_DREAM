What Is PDF to Word Conversion?

PDF to Word conversion takes the fixed, print-ready layout of a PDF document and transforms it into an editable DOCX file. The converted document preserves the original text content, basic formatting (headings, bold, italic), tables, and where possible, images and layout structure. The output can be opened and edited in Microsoft Word, Google Docs, LibreOffice Writer, or any compatible word processor.

How to Convert PDF to Word on PDFWorks

  2. Upload your PDF by dragging it onto the upload zone or clicking to browse. Maximum size: 20 MB free (50 MB Pro).
  3. Click Convert. Conversion takes 5–60 seconds depending on document length and complexity.
  4. Download the resulting .docx file.

What Converts Well

Our converter works best with digitally-created PDFs (not scanned images). It accurately preserves:

  • Paragraph text and basic formatting (bold, italic, underline)
  • Heading levels
  • Bulleted and numbered lists
  • Simple tables
  • Hyperlinks
  • Page layout (margins and columns in many cases)

Limitations to Be Aware Of

PDF-to-Word conversion is technically challenging — PDF stores visual output, not document structure. Some elements may need manual correction:

  • Complex multi-column layouts may be simplified
  • Custom fonts may be substituted if not installed on your system
  • Complex tables with merged cells may need reformatting
  • Scanned PDFs (image-only) require OCR first — use our Document → OCR tool
