Best Free PDF Tools in 2024: Complete Comparison

March 20, 20249 min read

The market for PDF tools is vast — ranging from fully free browser-based utilities to expensive professional desktop suites. For most users who just need to compress a PDF, convert a document, or merge some files, a free tool does the job perfectly. This guide reviews the main options honestly: what they're good at, where they fall short, and what to watch out for in terms of privacy and data handling.

What to Look for in a Free PDF Tool

Before diving into specific tools, here are the key factors to evaluate:

  • Feature completeness — Does it do what you need, or do you have to upgrade to paid tiers for basic features?
  • Privacy and data handling — Does the tool store your files? Require an account? Share data with third parties?
  • File size limits — Many free tools impose severe limits (5–10 MB) that make them impractical for real-world use.
  • Output quality — Compression and conversion quality varies widely between tools.
  • Speed and reliability — Online tools should process files quickly without errors.
  • No watermarks — Some "free" tools add watermarks to output files, making them unusable without purchasing.

Online PDF Tools

PDFWorks (pdfworks.io)

PDFWorks is a privacy-first free online platform covering PDF, image, audio, video, and document conversion. Key characteristics:

  • Privacy: No account required. Files deleted automatically in 30 minutes. No tracking cookies.
  • PDF features: Compress, convert to/from Word and Excel, merge, split, unlock, strip metadata, PDF to images, images to PDF, PPT to PDF.
  • File limit: 20 MB free, 50 MB with Pro token ($5 for 30 days).
  • Output: No watermarks. Clean output.
  • Best for: Users who prioritise privacy and need a comprehensive set of tools without signing up.

Smallpdf

One of the most popular online PDF platforms, with a wide range of tools. However, the free tier is limited:

  • Free users can complete two tasks per hour.
  • Requires account creation for full features.
  • Files are stored on their servers for 1 hour.
  • Paid plans start at around €9/month.

Best for: Occasional use where task limits aren't a problem and you don't mind the account requirement.

ILovePDF

A solid set of PDF tools with a clean interface. Free tier includes most tools but has file size limits and requires an account for batch operations. Ad-supported. Output quality is generally good.

Best for: Users who need a variety of PDF tools and don't mind signing up.

PDF2Go

German-based tool with good coverage of features. Privacy policy indicates files are deleted after some hours. Has a reasonable free tier. Interface is somewhat cluttered with ads.

Best for: European users who want GDPR-compliant processing.

Sejda PDF

Clean interface, good feature set. Free tier allows processing up to 3 tasks per hour with files up to 50 MB. Desktop version also available. Privacy policy states files are deleted after 2 hours.

Best for: Users who occasionally need a 50 MB limit without paying.

Desktop PDF Tools (Free)

LibreOffice (Writer + Draw)

The open-source office suite includes robust PDF export and basic PDF editing via LibreOffice Draw. You can open a PDF in Draw, edit text and images (with some limitations), and re-export as PDF. It supports PDF/A export, font embedding, and encryption.

Pros: Fully offline, no data leaves your computer, highly capable. Cons: PDF editing is cumbersome; text reflow isn't handled well for complex layouts.

Best for: Privacy-conscious users who need to edit PDFs without cloud tools, and already use LibreOffice.

PDF24 Creator

A free Windows desktop application with surprisingly comprehensive features: merge, split, compress, convert, OCR, and more. The desktop version is completely free with no watermarks. Also available as an online tool.

Best for: Windows users who want an offline, free, all-in-one PDF tool.

Okular (Linux/KDE)

A powerful document viewer for Linux with PDF annotation support. More of a viewer/annotator than an editor, but it handles PDF viewing, form filling, and annotations well.

Best for: Linux users who need a capable PDF viewer with basic editing.

Preview (macOS)

The built-in macOS app is more capable than many people realise. It can merge PDFs (by dragging pages in the sidebar), annotate, fill forms, sign documents, and export with password protection. No third-party software needed for many common tasks.

Best for: Mac users who need quick PDF operations without installing anything.

What About Adobe Acrobat?

Adobe Acrobat is the gold standard for PDF editing and creation. Acrobat Reader (for viewing, basic form filling, and commenting) is free. Acrobat Pro (for editing, OCR, advanced tools) costs around €20/month. For power users who work with PDFs professionally every day, it's worth the cost. For occasional use, free tools like those above are more than sufficient.

Privacy Warning: What Happens to Your Files?

When using online PDF tools, your files are uploaded to third-party servers. This matters for sensitive documents. Before using any online tool for confidential content:

  • Check the privacy policy for how long files are stored.
  • Look for explicit confirmation that files are not shared with third parties.
  • Consider whether the document contains personal data, trade secrets, or legally privileged information.
  • For maximum privacy, use an offline tool (LibreOffice, PDF24) for sensitive documents.

PDFWorks deletes files within 30 minutes and requires no account — but for truly sensitive documents, we still recommend considering an offline alternative.
